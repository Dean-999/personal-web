— My Swimming Journey
My story with swimming began as an unexpected encounter. If you ever saw a picture of me when I was little, you would see a chubby and innocent boy who was scared to take off his shirt in front of others because everyone laughed at my big belly. I was also afraid of drowning, so swimming definitely didn't seem like a good choice for me.
The turning point came during a family trip with one of my mother's friends. Her son, who was older than me and an excellent swimmer, went with us. When we arrived at the hotel pool, I sat on the edge and refused to go in no matter how people tried to persuade me. Suddenly, that boy lifted me from behind and threw me into the water. As someone who didn't know how to swim, I panicked and splashed around, trying desperately to get out. But he quickly pulled me up, held my hands, and guided me slowly through the water to help me relax and feel the fun of it. Surprisingly, my fear began to fade, and I even felt… comfortable. This makes me have interest in swimming and lets me actually try swimming.
After receiving systematic swimming training, I eventually got rid of my big belly and even developed a bit of shape. Swimming not only improved my physical health but also made me more confident to get better and better game results. Long-term practice helped me concentrate better in class, easily pass physical fitness tests, and taught me resilience and persistence. Swimming showed me the importance of sports and how much it can positively shape my life. I think this is the most valuable lesson I learn and an important reason for me to stick with swimming for the rest of my life.
