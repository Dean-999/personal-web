My Story

My journey in robotics design began when I joined the FTC team, and it quickly became one of the most challenging yet rewarding experiences of my high school years. As the designer, I learned that creating a successful robot isn't just about technical skills—it's about understanding the competition, working under pressure, and collaborating with teammates who bring different perspectives. What I love most is the problem-solving aspect: analyzing requirements, thinking creatively about solutions, and seeing those ideas come to life through 3D modeling and printing. Through this experience, I've learned that failure is just another step toward success, and that the best designs often come from learning what doesn't work.

My Story

Once I had a journey to a waterfall, I was attracted by the shining rocks. I picked it up—it was translucent and kind of rounded. You know I was so excited that time and I thought I found precious jewellery. I brought it back home and asked my father's friend, who is a geology expert. He told me it is just a piece of glass. I was shocked at that moment and felt really down. Because I already told my classmates that I am becoming a millionaire and I am gonna start my journey travelling around the world tomorrow. And you tell me it is just a piece of glass that only costs 1 dollar? NO WAY! But still, these elegant rocks left me wondering, and that curiosity grew into a deep passion for minerals that has shaped much of my learning journey.
